# SECTION 1: PROGRAMMING FOR ONLINE

## What Changed from In-Person

### Key Differences
- **No real-time cueing** — You won't see them during the set
- **Video check-ins** — Clients film and send via TrueCoach
- **Self-assessment focus** — Clients must learn to feel correct movement
- **Simpler movements** — Fewer complex lifts requiring immediate correction
- **Higher accountability** — Clearer check-in protocols essential

### Programming Adjustments
- Prioritize movements that are hard to mess up
- More regressions readily available
- Clear self-check criteria before advancing
- Weekly video submission required
- Program designed for 80% autonomous completion

---

# SECTION 2: CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Safety Through Simplicity
Choose movements with wide margin of error. If they can't do it perfectly solo, it doesn't go in the program.

### 2. Self-Assessment Before Video
Clients must answer: "Can you do this without pain or compensation?" BEFORE filming.

### 3. Video Quality Over Quantity
One good video beats 10 blurry ones. Teach clients to film smart.

### 4. Weekly Check-Ins
No more than 7 days between video submissions. The gap between correct and incorrect practice must be closed quickly.

### 5. The 3-Rep Rule
If client feels any pain/discomfort during 3 reps, stop. Film and send before continuing.

# SECTION 4: THE SCALING SYSTEM (Online-Optimized)

### Tier 1: FOUNDATION (New to Online)
- Very simple movements
- High confidence in self-assessment
- Weekly video required
- More frequent check-ins (2x/week)

### Tier 2: DEVELOPMENT (Comfortable with Online)
- Moderate complexity
- Reliable self-assessment
- Weekly video sufficient
- Standard check-ins (1x/week)

### Tier 3: PERFORMANCE (Independent)
- Can handle more complex cues
- Excellent form over time
- Bi-weekly video OK
- Monthly consultation

# SECTION 5: ONLINE PROGRESSION TRIGGERS

## The Video Submission Protocol

### Every Week, Client Must Submit:
1. **One working set** of each main movement (filmed)
2. **Self-assessment:** Rate form 1-10 with explanation
3. **Pain check:** Any discomfort? Where?
4. **Question:** One thing to improve

### Progression Decision Tree

```
FRIDAY: Client submits videos
   ↓
YOU REVIEW: Watch each video
   ↓
FORM ISSUES? ──YES──→ Send correction, stay at weight
   ↓
NO
   ↓
ALL MOVEMENTS ≥ 8/10? ──NO──→ Same weight next week
   ↓
YES
   ↓
3 WEEKS IN A ROW ≥ 8/10? ──NO──→ Add weight or reps
   ↓
YES
   ↓
ADVANCE TO NEXT LEVEL
```

### What You're Looking For on Video

**Good Signs:**
- Full range of motion
- Controlled tempo
- Steady posture
- No visible grimace/pain
- Smooth transitions

**Red Flags:**
- Squinting/grimacing
- Movement substitution
- Range of motion loss
- Visible compensation
- Resting between reps longer than usual

# SECTION 6: PROGRAM STRUCTURE (12-Week Online)

### Block 1: FOUNDATION (Weeks 1-4)
**Goal:** Establish movement patterns, build self-assessment skill

| Week | Focus | Video Requirement |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| 1 | Technique | 1 video per movement (all 5 patterns) |
| 2 | Load | 1 video per main movement |
| 3 | Volume | 1 video, self-rate each movement |
| 4 | Test | 1 video, assessment summary |

### Block 2: STRENGTH (Weeks 5-8)
**Goal:** Build load capacity

| Week | Focus | Video Requirement |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| 5 | 5x5 pattern | 1 video, hardest set |
| 6 | 5x5 + accessory | Main + 1 accessory |
| 7 | Heavy 3x5 | Focus video on form |
| 8 | Deload | 1 video, lighter weights |

### Block 3: CONDITIONING (Weeks 9-12)
**Goal:** Combine strength with work capacity

| Week | Focus | Video Requirement |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| 9 | Strength + conditioning | Video of circuit |
| 10 | Heavy + EMOM | Video main lifts |
| 11 | Peaking | Video heaviest day |
| 12 | Assessment | Full video submission |

# SECTION 9: CLIENT VIDEO GUIDE

### How to Film for Dave (Copy/Paste to Send)

**Camera Position:**
- Landscape mode (phone sideways)
- Far enough back to see full body
- Good lighting (face the light, not the window)

**What to Film:**
- Complete set with rest built in
- Pause at bottom of each rep
- Walk away from camera at end (show full gait)

**Good Example:**
"Here's Monday's KB deadlift. 3x8 at 24 lb. Form felt solid, no hip shift this time."

**Bad Example:**
"Here it is" [blurry, 2 seconds, can't see anything]

# SECTION 10: NO-PAIN PROTOCOL (ONLINE VERSION)

### Pre-Workout Self-Check
Client rates each body part 0-10:
- Shoulders: _
- Lower Back: _
- Hips: _
- Knees: _

**If any 4-6:** Reduce load 20%
**If any 7+:** Skip exercise, substitute, message Dave immediately

### During-Set Rule
**3 Rep Rule:** If something feels off for 3 consecutive reps, STOP. Film it. Send it. Wait for response.

### Post-Workout
- Can you get up off the floor without using hands?
- Any new pain since starting?
- Did anything feel "off" that wasn't painful?

# SECTION 11: HANDOFF TOOLS

### When Client is Ready for In-Person
If client eventually comes to studio, the transition is natural:
- Same movement patterns
- Same scaling tiers
- Same progression triggers
- Just add load and real-time coaching

### Scale Up Path
1. Online: Tier 3, max KB
2. Studio: Introduce barbell variations
3. Hybrid: Online programming + in-person check-ins
4. Full studio: Full STRONGVILITY 2.0 system
