# STRONGVILITY
## A Kettlebell-Based Training System for Adults 55+
### Designed for Semi-Private Groups with Orthopedic Considerations

---

# SECTION 1: PROGRAMMING PHILOSOPHY

## Core Principles

### 1. Joint-Friendly But Effective
Every movement chosen prioritizes joint integrity over ego. The goal is sustainable strength, not max performance. We build tissue capacity before adding load.

### 2. Auto-Regulated Progression
The system uses performance triggers rather than calendar-based progression. A client moves up when they demonstrate readiness — not because it's "Week 4."

### 3. Scalable for Groups
The same movement can be regressed or progressed for each individual within a semi-private setting. One KB, multiple levels.

### 4. Tissue Tolerance Over Artifacts
We don't chase PRs. We chase "can I do this without pain tomorrow?" If a client can't recover from a session, the system fails.

### 5. The Big 6 Movement Patterns
Every session includes elements from:
- Pressing
- Pulling
- Hinging
- Squatting
- Gait/Carrying
- Rotation/Stability

# SECTION 2: THE MOVEMENT LIBRARY

## A. PRESSING (Upper Body Push)

| Movement | Regression | Standard | Progression | Orthopedic Note |
|----------|------------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Half-Kneeling Press | No weight, vertical hand | KB racked position | Standing Press | Shoulder-friendly, core-engaged |
| Floor Press | Lighter KB, both hands | Single arm | Weighted push-up | Zero shoulder compression |
| Wall Push-Up | Hands on elevated surface | Standard wall push-up | Decline push-up | Gradual load increase |
| Landmine Press | No weight | Single arm | Double arm | Rotation-friendly for shoulder |

### Pressing Performance Triggers
- Move up when: Client can complete all prescribed reps with perfect form and RPE ≤ 7
- Add reps (8→10→12) before adding weight
- Add weight (2-4 lb increments) when 12 reps are easy

---

## B. PULLING (Upper Body Pull)

| Movement | Regression | Standard | Progression | Orthopedic Note |
|----------|------------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Single-Arm KB Row | Chest supported on bench | Bent over single arm | Standing single arm | Lower back safe |
| Pull-Apart | Resistance band | Light KB | Heavier KB | Rotator cuff friendly |
| Inverted Row | Horizontal body angle (feet elevated) | Standard inverted row | Weighted inverted row | No shoulder impingement |
| Doorframe/PG Row | Holding doorframe | Band around pull-up bar | Single arm row | Back-friendly |

### Pulling Performance Triggers
- Move up when: Full range of motion with control, no lumbar flexion
- Add reps first, then weight
- Watch for shoulder compensation

---

## C. HINGING (Hip Hinge)

| Movement | Regression | Standard | Progression | Orthopedic Note |
|----------|------------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| KB Deadlift | Boxedal, light KB | Standard KB deadlift | Single leg RDL | Best for lower back health |
| RDL (Romanian) | Hold onto door/band | KB behind (around legs) | Single leg RDL | Hip hinge not lumbar hinge |
| Swing | Light KB, focuses on hinge | Two-arm swing | Single-arm swing | Decompresses spine |
| Swings to Reps | 10 reps EMOM | 15 reps EMOM | 20 reps EMOM | Cardio + strength |

### Hinging Performance Triggers
- Move up when: Neutral spine throughout, full hip hinge depth
- Watch for:
  - Rounded lower back
  - Knee cave
  - Heels lifting
- Add weight when form is pristine for 10+ reps

---

## D. SQUATTING (Lower Body)

| Movement | Regression | Standard | Progression | Orthopedic Note |
|----------|------------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Air Squat | Hold onto door/band | Bodyweight only | Goblet squat | Knee-friendly |
| Goblet Squat | Light KB | Standard KB | Front rack position | Loads the quads |
| Box Squat | Higher box, lighter KB | Lower box | No box | Knee-safe depth control |
| Assisted Squat | Hold onto band/door | Bodyweight | Tempo squat (3 sec down) | Forces control |

### Squatting Performance Triggers
- Move up when: Full depth (thighs below parallel), knees tracking over toes
- Progression order: Reps → Tempo → Weight
- Regression if: Knee pain, heel lift, lumbar rounding

---

## E. GAIT/CARRYING (Functional Carry)

| Movement | Regression | Standard | Progression | Orthopedic Note |
|----------|------------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Suitcase Carry | Lighter KB, shorter distance | Standard suitcase | Overhead walk | Anti-rotation |
| Farmer's Carry | Lighter KB, shorter distance | Standard farmer's | Offset carry | Anti-lateral flexion |
| Rucking | Bodyweight only | Light sandbag | Heavier ruck | Gradual loading |
| Lateral Shuffle | Non-weighted | Weight at sides | Weight across chest | Hip mobility |

### Gait Performance Triggers
- Move up when: Upright posture, steady gait, no compensation
- Increase distance before adding weight
- Watch for: Hip drop, trunk lean, knee valgus

---

## F. ROTATION & STABILITY

| Movement | Regression | Standard | Progression | Orthopedic Note |
|----------|------------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Palloff Press | No weight, hold 5 sec | KB hold at chest | Band or heavier KB | Anti-rotation core |
| Woodchop | Low to high | High to low | Single arm | Hip rotation + core |
| Russian Twist | No weight | KB or plate | Weighted rotation | Controlled speed |
| Rotational Lunge | No weight | KB at chest | Weighted rotation | Hip + knee friendly |

### Rotation Performance Triggers
- Move up when: No spinal twist, controlled hip hinge
- Watch for: Knee valgus, lower back rotation
- Hold positions longer before adding load

# SECTION 3: THE SCALING SYSTEM

## Three Tiers Within Groups

### TIER 1: FOUNDATION
- New to KB training
- Significant orthopedic history (recent injury, chronic pain)
- Recently returned to training after extended break

### TIER 2: DEVELOPMENT
- 3-6 months KB experience
- Orthopedic issues managed, not resolved
- Ready for moderate load/volume

### TIER 3: PERFORMANCE
- 6+ months consistent KB training
- Orthopedic issues stable
- Ready for higher intensity

## How to Place Clients

| Observation | Place In |
|-------------|----------|
| Can't squat to depth without knee cave | Tier 1 |
| Shoulder pain with overhead press | Tier 1 or regress movement |
| 6+ months training, no issues | Tier 3 |
| Back pain with hinging | Tier 1 or regress to rack positions |
| RPE consistently 9-10 | Deload or regress |
| RPE 6-7, easy | Progress |

# SECTION 4: 12-WEEK BLOCK SYSTEM

## Block Structure

### Block 1: FOUNDATION (Weeks 1-4)
**Goal:** Establish movement patterns, build tissue tolerance, learn the system

| Week | Focus | Volume | Intensity |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Technique only | 8-10 reps per exercise | RPE 5-6 |
| 2 | Add load | 10-12 reps | RPE 6-7 |
| 3 | Build volume | 12-15 reps | RPE 7 |
| 4 | Test week | 3x8 reps | RPE 7-8 |

**Key Metrics to Test:**
- Get-Up Test (time to get up from floor)
- 5-rep max on KB deadlift
- Farmer's carry distance (40 lb)
- Push-up hold time

---

### Block 2: STRENGTH (Weeks 5-8)
**Goal:** Build absolute strength, introduce intensity cycles

| Week | Focus | Volume | Intensity |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| 5 | Strength 5x5 | 5x5 | RPE 7-8 |
| 6 | Strength 5x5 + accessory | 5x5 + 3x10 | RPE 8 |
| 7 | Strength 3x5 heavy | 3x5 | RPE 8-9 |
| 8 | Deload | 50% volume | RPE 5-6 |

**Key Metrics to Test:**
- 5-rep max on goblet squat
- 5-rep max on half-kneeling press
- Push-up test (max in 2 min)
- Plank hold time

---

### Block 3: CONDITIONING + STRENGTH (Weeks 9-12)
**Goal:** Combine strength with work capacity, peak for assessment

| Week | Focus | Volume | Intensity |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| 9 | Strength + conditioning | 4x6 + EMOM | RPE 8 |
| 10 | Strength + conditioning | 4x6 + EMOM | RPE 8-9 |
| 11 | Peaking | 3x5 heavy | RPE 9 |
| 12 | TEST DAY | Assessment | Max effort |

**Assessment Tests:**
- KB deadlift 5RM
- Push-up max reps
- Farmer's carry 60 sec
- Get-up time
- Body composition (waist + weight)

# SECTION 5: PROGRESSON TRIGGERS (DECISION TREE)

## The Performance Trigger System

### For Every Exercise, Check These Boxes Before Progressing:

#### 1. FORM CHECK
- [ ] Neutral spine
- [ ] Knee tracking over toes
- [ ] Full range of motion
- [ ] No pain during movement
- [ ] No compensation

**If ANY box unchecked → DO NOT PROGRESS**

#### 2. REPETITION READINESS
- [ ] Can complete all prescribed reps with good form
- [ ] RPE ≤ 7 on final set
- [ ] Recovery completed between sets (< 3 min)

**If ALL boxes checked → ADD REPS**

#### 3. LOAD READINESS
- [ ] 12+ reps completed with RPE ≤ 7
- [ ] 3 consecutive sessions at this rep range
- [ ] No regression in form with fatigue
- [ ] Client reports "could do 3-4 more"

**If ALL boxes checked → ADD WEIGHT**

---

## Progression Decision Tree

```
START: Client finishes prescribed reps
   ↓
FORM PERFECT? ──NO──→ Repeat current weight, fix form
   ↓
YES
   ↓
RPE ≤ 7? ──NO──→ Same weight next session
   ↓
YES
   ↓
12 REPS COMPLETED? ──NO──→ Add reps (up to 12)
   ↓
YES
   ↓
3 SESSIONS AT THIS WEIGHT? ──NO──→ Same weight next 1-2 sessions
   ↓
YES
   ↓
ADD 2-4 LB (KB) OR 1-2 REP (BODYWEIGHT)
```

---

## Regression Triggers (When to Drop Weight)

- RPE ≥ 9 on final set
- Form breakdown during reps
- Client reports pain (during or after)
- Heart rate doesn't recover within 2 minutes
- Sleep quality affected negatively

**When any regression trigger hits:**
→ Drop weight by 10-15%
→ Re-test at current rep range
→ Work back up over 2-3 sessions

# SECTION 8: KEY EXERCISE DESCRIPTIONS

## THE GET-UP (Turkish Get-Up)
**Why:** Total body stability, shoulder health, floor recovery
**Regression:** Floor only, no weight → light KB → standard
**Progression:** Faster time, heavier KB

## THE SWING (Russian)
**Why:** Hip hinge power, posterior chain, conditioning
**Regression:** Two hands, lighter weight
**Progression:** Single arm, heavier weight

## THE GOBLET SQUAT
**Why:** Quad dominant, knee stability, depth work
**Regression:** Box, hold onto band
**Progression:** Front rack, heavier weight

## THE SINGLE ARM ROW
**Why:** Pulling strength, scapular health, anti-rotation
**Regression:** Bent knee, lighter weight
**Progression:** Standing, heavier weight

## THE RACKED CARRY (Suitcase)
**Why:** Anti-lateral flexion, grip strength, core stability
**Regression:** Lighter KB, shorter distance
**Progression:** Heavier KB, overhead variations

# SECTION 9: IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR COACHES

## Group Coaching Cues

### In a Semi-Private Setting:
1. **Demo the movement** before they start
2. **Watch each person** during first few reps
3. **Use tier system** — call out "Tier 2, add 4 more reps"
4. **Simplify queues** — "Knees out," "Chest up," "Hips back"
5. **Manage rest** — "Rest 60 seconds, begin in 3, 2, 1..."

### Communication Templates:
- "Tier 1, we're staying with 8 pounds"
- "Tier 3, let's see 12 reps today"
- "Everyone take a breath, we're going to the floor"
- "Check in — how does this feel in your shoulder?"

## Programming for 2-Day vs 3-Day Split

### 2-Day Format (Semi-Private)
- Session A + Session B alternate
- 3x per week = A-B-A, B-A-B

### 3-Day Format
- A-B-C pattern rotated
- 3x per week = A-B-C, A-B-C

# APPENDIX B: ORTHOPEDIC MODIFICATIONS QUICK REFERENCE

| Issue | Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Shoulder impingement | Overhead press > 90° | Half-kneeling, floor press |
| Lower back pain | Heavy hinges, loaded rotations | Goblet squat, rack position |
| Knee issues | Deep lunges, heavy squats | Box squat, reverse lunges |
| Hip issues | Deep hip hinges | Box squat, banded walks |
| Wrist issues | Front rack hold | Racked position, goblet |

# APPENDIX C: THE "NO PAIN" PROTOCOL

## Pre-Session Screening (30 seconds)

Ask client: "On a scale of 0-10, how does your [shoulder/back/knee] feel?"
- 0-3: Proceed normally
- 4-6: Reduce load by 20%, watch form
- 7+: Skip exercise, substitute something pain-free

## During Session Check-Ins
- After warm-up: "How are we feeling?"
- After strength set: "Anything I need to know about?"
- Before adding weight: "Ready for more?"

## Post-Session Rules
- Client must be able to do the Get-Up test tomorrow
- If they can't get up from floor, session was too intense
- Soreness should be "worked" out, not "worked" to injury
