# SECTION 1: PROGRAMMING PHILOSOPHY

## Core Principles

### 1. Joint Integrity First
Every movement chosen prioritizes joint health over load. We build capacity before adding weight.

### 2. Auto-Regulated Progression
Performance triggers — not calendar dates — determine when clients advance.

### 3. Tool Agnostic, Results Focused
Use whatever tool fits the client. The system adapts to your equipment, not the other way around.

### 4. Tissue Tolerance Over Egos
"Can I do this without pain tomorrow?" If no, the programming fails.

### 5. The Big 7 Movement Patterns
Every session includes:
- Push (horizontal + vertical)
- Pull (horizontal + vertical)
- Hinge (hip dominant)
- Squat (knee dominant)
- Carry (gait/functional)
- Rotate (anti-rotation + rotational)
- Accessory/Isolation (local muscle work)

# SECTION 3: SCALING SYSTEM

## Three Tiers

### TIER 1: FOUNDATION
- New to resistance training
- Significant orthopedic history
- Recently returned to training

### TIER 2: DEVELOPMENT
- 3-6 months consistent training
- Orthopedic issues managed
- Ready for moderate load/volume

### TIER 3: PERFORMANCE
- 6+ months consistent training
- Orthopedic issues stable
- Ready for higher intensity

# SECTION 4: 12-WEEK BLOCK SYSTEM

## Block 1: FOUNDATION (Weeks 1-4)
**Goal:** Movement patterns, tissue tolerance, learn the system

| Week | Focus | Volume | Intensity |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Technique only | 8-10 reps | RPE 5-6 |
| 2 | Add load | 10-12 reps | RPE 6-7 |
| 3 | Build volume | 12-15 reps | RPE 7 |
| 4 | Test week | 3x8 reps | RPE 7-8 |

## Block 2: STRENGTH (Weeks 5-8)
**Goal:** Build absolute strength, introduce intensity cycles

| Week | Focus | Volume | Intensity |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| 5 | Strength 5x5 | 5x5 | RPE 7-8 |
| 6 | Strength 5x5 + accessory | 5x5 + 3x10 | RPE 8 |
| 7 | Strength 3x5 heavy | 3x5 | RPE 8-9 |
| 8 | Deload | 50% volume | RPE 5-6 |

## Block 3: CONDITIONING + PEAK (Weeks 9-12)
**Goal:** Combine strength with work capacity, assess

| Week | Focus | Volume | Intensity |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| 9 | Strength + conditioning | 4x6 + EMOM | RPE 8 |
| 10 | Strength + conditioning | 4x6 + EMOM | RPE 8-9 |
| 11 | Peaking | 3x5 heavy | RPE 9 |
| 12 | TEST DAY | Assessment | Max effort |

# SECTION 5: PROGRESSON TRIGGERS

## Decision Tree

```
Client finishes prescribed reps
   ↓
FORM PERFECT? ──NO──→ Repeat weight, fix form
   ↓
YES
   ↓
RPE ≤ 7? ──NO──→ Same weight next session
   ↓
YES
   ↓
12 REPS COMPLETED? ──NO──→ Add reps (up to 12)
   ↓
YES
   ↓
3 SESSIONS AT THIS WEIGHT? ──NO──→ Stay at weight
   ↓
YES
   ↓
ADD WEIGHT OR REPS
```

## Regression Triggers
- RPE ≥ 9 on final set
- Form breakdown
- Client reports pain
- 2+ days recovery needed

# SECTION 7: NO PAIN PROTOCOL

## Pre-Session Screening
Ask: "On a scale of 0-10, how does your [body part] feel?"
- 0-3: Proceed normally
- 4-6: Reduce load 20%, watch form
- 7+: Skip exercise, substitute

## During Session Check-Ins
- After warm-up: "How are we feeling?"
- After strength set: "Anything I need to know?"
- Before adding weight: "Ready for more?"

## Post-Session Rules
- Client must be able to get up from floor (Get-Up test)
- Soreness should be "worked out" not "worked to"

# APPENDIX B: ORTHOPEDIC MODIFICATIONS

| Issue | Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Shoulder impingement | Overhead press >90° | Landmine, floor press, push-up |
| Lower back pain | Heavy hinges, heavy squat | Goblet squat, rack position, hip thrust |
| Knee issues | Deep lunges, heavy squat | Box squat, leg press, step-up |
| Hip issues | Deep hinges | Hip thrust, glute bridge, band walks |
| Wrist issues | Front rack | Monkey handles, goblet, neutral grip |

# APPENDIX C: IMPLEMENTATION FOR COACHES

## Group Cues
- "Tier 2, add 4 more reps"
- "Check in — how does this feel?"
- "Rest 60 seconds, begin in 3, 2, 1..."

## 2-Day vs 3-Day Split
- 2-Day: Session A + B alternate (A-B-A, B-A-B)
- 3-Day: A-B-C rotate (A-B-C, A-B-C)
